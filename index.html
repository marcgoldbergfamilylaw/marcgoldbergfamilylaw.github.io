    About Me

    Biography

    I grew up, and have lived in the San Fernando Valley, my entire life. I knew from the time I was 14 years old that I wanted to become an attorney. My 9 th grade history teacher wanted us to learn about the legal system and had us put on a mock trial. I was fortunate enough to be chosen as one of the attorneys. My partner and I won the case. The teacher’s husband, who is an attorney, played the judge. He came to me after the trial and told me that I should think about practicing law as a career, He paid me a very high compliment by telling me “You are a natural”. From that moment on I knew my life’s calling.

    I attended California State University, Northridge, where I graduated in 1986 with my Bachelor of Arts Degree in Political Science and a minor in History. While at CSUN I joined Zeta Beta Tau Fraternity, where I made many lifetime friends and connections.

    After CSUN, I attended law school at Southwestern University College of Law, in Los Angeles. I graduated from Southwestern in 1990. I passed the California State Bar Examination in 1991 and began practicing law, first in the field of Plaintiff Personal Injury law.

    After going through my own divorce, I was encouraged by my attorney to open a Family Law practice. The Law Offices of Marc D. Goldberg has been in operation, serving the needs of family law litigants since 2003. The focus of my law practice, from the very beginning, has been family law. Currently, I have been exclusively practicing Family Law for 17 years.

    I am the father of two daughter, ages 23 and 20, who also now attend CSUN. One is a Child Development major and the other is a Psychology major.

    I am still as excited by the idea of practicing law and helping people who are going through some of the most stressful times of their lives as I was the day my 9 th grade teacher’s husband first encouraged me to become an attorney. I am a fierce advocate for my clients and always seek, for them, the best possible results that the law allows.

    MY PHILOSOPHY OF PRACTING LAW

    I have always had a very strong sense of justice. Whether it was someone being bullied on a playground, or some news article where a person was not treated fairly by a business or the government, my heart has always been with the underdog. As we all do, there were times when I was young, where I felt I had not been treated with fairness and dignity. These ideals set me on a course to ensure that people are treated fairly. I knew that the best way to see these ideals met was in the practice of law.

    Today, whether it be a person going through a contentious property battle in a divorce, a parent fighting for the right to spend time with his or her child , or the survivor of domestic violence seeking protection from their abuser, I will take all necessary actions to obtain the best possible result, for my client, that the law provides.

    I am strong believer in transparency with my clients to ensure that, not only do they know what legal actions I am taking on behalf, but to thoroughly explain to them why I am taking those actions. You spend a lot of money when you have to retain an attorney to represent your interests in Family Court, and I believe that you should absolutely understand every aspect of the actions being taken to protect your legal rights. I will always be there to answer your questions and to make sure that you are satisfied with the answers you receive.

    Finally, I will always handle your case in a timely manner. I do not believe in unnecessarily prolonging a case. I strongly believe in settlement, where possible. But there are times when settlement cannot be reached, and a case must be litigated. My decades of legal knowledge and experience in Court allows me to present your case in the most legally favorable light possible.

    When you retain The Law Offices of Marc D. Goldberg to handle your Family Law matter, you can rest assured that you will be treated fairly and with dignity, and you will receive experienced and knowledgeable legal advice and action.
